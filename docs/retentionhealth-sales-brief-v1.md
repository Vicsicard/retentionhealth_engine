## 1. The Market Context (Why This Matters Now)

### The GLP-1 Explosion

GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide, tirzepatide) represent the fastest-growing category in aesthetic and weight loss medicine. The US market is multi-billion dollars and expanding at 40%+ annually. What began as diabetes medication has become the most demanded weight loss intervention in modern medicine.

Independent medspas and medical weight loss clinics are adopting GLP-1 programs aggressively. Patient demand is unprecedented. Acquisition is not the problem.

### The Hidden Economic Crisis

**30-40% of GLP-1 patients drop off within the first 60 days.**

This is not anecdotal. This is the industry pattern. And most clinics don't measure it scientifically.

Early churn destroys lifetime value. A single GLP-1 patient retained for 12 months represents $7,200+ in recurring revenue (at $600/month average). Losing 12 patients to early drop-off costs a clinic $86,400 annually.

For a clinic with 120 active GLP-1 patients experiencing 30% baseline drop-off, that's **36 lost patients = $259,200 in lost annual revenue.**

### Why Clinics Are Bleeding Revenue

The problem is not patient acquisition. It's **titration volatility.**

During the first 8 weeks, patients experience:
- Nausea and food-related distress
- Unpredictable side effects
- Confusion about dosing progression
- Anxiety about "doing it wrong"

Clinics respond with:
- Reactive support calls
- Staff overwhelm
- Inconsistent guidance
- No structured reinforcement

The result:
- Patients ghost
- Refills become unpredictable
- MRR volatility
- Staff burnout

**This is not a marketing problem. It's a stabilization problem.**

**The market needs a retention layer, not another feature.**

## 4. The Pilot (What and Why)

**Target:** 6-10 independent GLP-1 clinics
